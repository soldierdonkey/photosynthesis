Light dependent reactions:

Sunlight energy (a yellow photon) hits photosystem 2 (green box), exciting the electrons and moving them into electron carriers, and then down the electron transport chain (teal boxes).

Water (H2O) then breaks up to refill the missing electrons. This water breaks down into two H+ ions and an Oxygen atom.

The energy from the electrons pump H+ ions from the stroma down to the thylakoid space.

The hydrogen ions are used to spin the ATP Synthase (tall red tower), which converts ADP to ATP. ATP carries energy that is later used in the calvin cycle.

At the end of the electron transport chain, the excess electrons combine with NADP+ to produce NADPH, an electron carrier. These electrons will later be used in the calvin cycle.

Calvin Cycle:

An enzyme called RuBisCo combines—or fixates—CO2, a 1 carbon molecule onto an RuBP, an 5 carbon molecule to make 2 3-carbon molecules called PGA. The leftover 2 Oxygen become byproducts. This step is called carbon fixation.

The RuBisCo is not consumed, as it is a catalyst. It is reused.

ATP and NADPH give energy and electrons to turn PGA into G3P. This step is called reduction.

5 G3P molecules turn into more RuBP molecules, and 1 goes to producing glucose. The process of turning G3P into RuBP is called regeneration, because the G3P is regenerating into RuBP.

Remember!

The balanced equation for photosynthesis is:
6 CO2 + 6 H2O → C6H12O6 + 6 O2
